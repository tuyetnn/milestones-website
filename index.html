    The First Digital Computer Invented.

    John Vincent Atanasoff, an American mathematician, and physicist. In 1937, he and his graduate student Cliff Berry started to discover and develop the first digital computer until 1942 at the Iowa State College. This is the first computer can store information on its main memory and it was the size of a big desk weighed 750 pounds and featured rotating drums for memory, glowing vacuum tubes.
