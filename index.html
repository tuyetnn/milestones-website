    • What Is The World Wide Web?

    The world wide web (WWW) can be known as the web, an information system where documents and other web resources are identified by Uniform Resource Locators. It is very easy to used and allow people to interactive with the other people to associate and share information over the world. Overall, the web contained the three key components and we are still using these remain today:

  • Hypertext Markup Language(HTML)-This is a standard markup language used to create web pages.
  • Uniform Resource Locator(URL)- This is the understandable form of a web address which is used to identify a resource.
  • Hyper Text Transfer Protocol(HTTP)-This protocol is the way for transferring files, such as text, graphic images,etc.

    How the world wide web started?

    Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist. He was born on June 8,1955 in London. After graduating from Oxford University, Berners-Lee became a software engineer at CERN Institute of Gevena, Switzerland. In 1989, he and his collegues began to introduce their invention to the world called "The world wide web" and published it in March. The world wide web is a system of hyperlink and hypertext documents accessed through the Internet. The purpose of this program was to make information readily accessible to the users. So they could sharing their idea and communicates with each other.

    The First Digital Computer Invented.

    John Vincent Atanasoff, an American mathematician, and physicist. In 1937, he and his graduate student, Cliff Berry started to discover and develop the first digital computer until 1942 at the Iowa State College. This was the first computer can store information on its main memory and it was the size of a big desk weighed 750 pounds and featured rotating drums for memory, glowing vacuum tubes.
